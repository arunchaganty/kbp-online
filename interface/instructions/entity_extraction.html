First of all, welcome :)

The focus of this task is to find mentions of entities in the given text. Entities can be people, organizations, places, dates or even titles given to people.

Here's a video to help it understand better. Remember you can always come back to this page by clicking on in the interface.

Steps

  1. As you read through the text, see if a word or a span of words strikes you as mentioning an entity (even if it is a pronoun, or referring indrectly e.g. "The president said ...").
  2. Upon finding a mention select it using your mouse. We've bolded spans which we guess might be useful, but we aren't that good and miss out on stuff.
  3. If you've never seen this entity in the text before, select its type via the Add new entity pane. You may then be taken to a dialogue box for further processing.
  4. If you have seen this entity then select it from Select entities to link panel

Correcting mistakes

  • If you feel you've made a mistake you can click on the incorrect span, the entity that you had selected would be highlighted.
  • If it was only a matter of you having selected a wrong pre-existing entity, it can be fixed by selecting the right one this time.
  • Otherwise, you can remove the span and redo the process correctly this time

Common mistakes

  1. It sometimes so happens that you end up creating a new entity instead of selecting one which you had already found.
  2. Pronouns are easy to miss.
  3. However do not highlight pronouns if you don't yet know who it refers to.
  4. Titles of people and dates are easy to miss too.
  5. Not looking at mentions that are not bolded

What is a title?

Official or unofficial name(s) of the employment or membership positions that is held by someone. Here are a few examples

Correct titles

  • Doctor, Lawyer, Founder
  • Chief Neurosurgeon, Associate Partner (the more specific, the better ;)

Incorrect titles

  • Hero, Socialite
  • " reporter with the LA Times" (no employer info required, just reporter is cool)

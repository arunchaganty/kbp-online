First of all, welcome :)

The focus of this task is to identify relationships between entities, i.e. people, organizations, places, dates and job titles, in the article. You will be shown a highlighted sentence with a red and a blue entity. We would like you to identify how the red and a blue entity are related, according to the highlighted sentence. It is also possible that they are unrelated or their relationship is not described in the highlighted sentence. Do not think about whether or not the two highlighted entities are related OUTSIDE this sentence.

Example

Consider the following example:

British prime minister Tony Blair addressed the gathering on Tuesday.
We would mark works for because the sentence identifies Tony Blair to be a minister for British. (Britain). Marking the relation lives at would be incorrect because the sentence doesn't explicitly mention this fact.

On the other hand, for,

Prime minister Tony Blair warned the public that some of those killed may be British.
we would mark the two to be unrelated because this sentence does not explicitly say that Tony Blair and British (Britain) are related.

Steps

  1. Choose a relation that holds between the red and the blue entities from the buttons below. On hovering over the button you can see a preview of the relation between them. Make sure that this relationship makes sense and is justified by the sentence.
  2. After you make your choice, you'll be given the next pair of entities.
  3. Once all relations have been extracted you'll find the done button activated.

What you also see

  1. All extracted relations are shown in the right pane for your reference. If you made a mistake, click on them and correct it.
  2. If you missed a relation, press the back button to review.

Common Pitfalls

  • If more than one relation seems to hold, pick the most appropriate one.
  • A relation may be true in general, but if it is not justified by the sentence, then it should be marked unrelated.
